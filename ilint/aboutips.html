THE INTERNATIONAL PROGRAMS AND STUDIES EXECUTIVE STAFF

Tim Barnes, Director, Illinois Strategic International Partnerships

Tim Barnes joined International Programs and Studies in 1998, and has worked in the offices of Overseas Projects and Foreign Visitors (OPFV), East Asian and Pacific Exchange Programs (EAEP), and Institutional and Faculty International Collaboration (IFIC) prior to assuming his current position as director of Illinois Strategic International Partnerships (ISIP). Tim now manages all formal institutional linkages between the Urbana-Champaign campus and international partners, as well as fostering increased international engagement among faculty and serving as the campus liaison officer for the Fulbright Senior Scholar program.

University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign International Student and Scholar Services
International Data Management Specialist
12 month, 75% position
Julia Bello Bravo, Assistant Director, Illinois Strategic International Partnerships

Dr. Julia Bello-Bravo joined International Programs and Studies in August of 2012 and serves as Assistant Director of the Illinois Strategic International Partnerships. Previously, she worked as an Extension Specialist in the area of educational programs for Hispanic/Latino populations in the state of Illinois and for low literate learners in developing nations. She has actively worked in the area of how to increase inclusiveness of under-represented people in educational networks and systems. As Assistant Director, her role is to promote Illinois engagement and partnerships in Africa and Latin America. She is also a co-founder of Scientific Animations Without BordersSM (SAWBOSM) and the Sustainable Development Virtual Knowledge Interface (SusDeViKISM) programs.

Primary Function:

This entry-level position will assist in managing data for international students to ensure compliance with federal regulations associated with the Student and Exchange Visitor Information System (SEVIS) and to provide statistical information for the University and related off-campus entities. The position also reconciles and reports on the unit budget.

She also serves as Affirmative Action Officer, Intake Specialist, and Departmental Classifier. Becky earned a BA in Liberal Studies from the University of Illinois-Springfield.

Duties and Responsibilities:
  • Assist with developing and restructuring internal processes for compatibility with Banner, existing University administrative systems, and federal regulations.
  • Provide administrative support for tracking of unit financial transactions.
Rebecca Blachly, Advancement Director

Rebecca joined IPS in 2011. Previously, she worked for the Principal Deputy Under Secretary of Defense for Policy, as the Acting Chief of the Strategic Communication Division at U.S. Africa Command, and as a research associate in the Post-Conflict Reconstruction Project at the Center for Strategic and International Studies (CSIS). Most recently, she completed her graduate work at Harvard, where she specialized in Christian-Muslim relations and the effect of religious dynamics on the UN peacekeeping mission in Sudan. She received her BA in philosophy from Williams College. Rebecca combines significant experience and a strong track record in grant writing and external funding with a deep understanding of international scholarship and engagement.

Duties and Responsibilities:
  • Assist International Data Coordinator in maintaining student and scholar database portion of fsaATLAS software program.
  • Provide logistical assistance for the collection of new international students for data entry and SEVIS-reporting obligations.
